Tusheti National Park - located on the Georgian - Chechen - Dagestani border, 42 km from Chateau Napareuli. The road leading to the park is one of the most beautiful routes in the world. The road was established in the 80' of the last century, through the Abano pass (2850 m.n.p.m.) leads to the largest village - Omalo. Visited places: Szenako, Dirtlo, Dartlo, Boczorna
Vashlovani National Park - located in the south-eastern corner of Kakheti region, 120 km from Chateau Napareuli. The unique nature of the park allows to admire nature from several climate zones. The steppe nature of this place contrasts with the view of the Caucas mountains. Visited places: Gorge of Eagle, mud volcanoes, gorge of Alazani river, Gorge of Bear.
Kazbegi National Park located in north-eastern Georgia, 180 km from Chateau Napareuli.          In the park, there is the third largest Georgian peak - Kazbeg (5047 m.n.p.m.).          Visited places: fortress in Sno, village of Juta, Chaukhi, Truso Valley, Darial Gorge, Gveleti waterfall, Cminda Sameba church.
Lagodekhi National Park - located in the eastern part of Kakheti, 77 km from Chateau Napareuli. This is the first national park in Georgia, founded in the 19th century by the Polish botanist Ludwik Młokosiewicz. It is full of unique specimens of fauna and flora: 121 species of plants occur only in the Caucasus region (9 of them only in Georgia). Visited places: Lagodekhi waterfall, Gurgeriana waterfall, Lake Black Rocks.
Wąwóz Pankisi - 44 km from Chateau Napareuli, a place inhabited by Georgian Chechens - Kists.      Visited places: Batsara, Babaneuri, Ilto nature reserves.
Lopota Waterfall – 15 km from Chateau Napareuli.
